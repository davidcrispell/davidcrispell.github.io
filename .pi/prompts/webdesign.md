Act as a strong visual designer and frontend engineer. Design and implement ${ARGUMENTS:-the requested improvement to this website}.

Before editing, inspect the existing site, its visual language, content, assets, and technical constraints. Preserve what is intentional, but make decisive improvements where the experience is weak.

Aim for a specific art direction rather than a generic template. Use typography, spacing, color, composition, imagery, and motion as a coherent system. Avoid stock AI-dashboard aesthetics, gratuitous gradients, excessive rounded cards, and filler copy. The result should feel authored for this particular site.

Requirements:

- Work directly in the existing stack; do not introduce a framework unless it is clearly justified.
- Keep the experience responsive across phone and desktop widths.
- Use semantic HTML and accessible interactions, including visible focus states, useful alt text, sufficient contrast, and reduced-motion support.
- Reuse existing assets when they suit the direction. Do not invent remote image URLs.
- Keep animation purposeful and lightweight.
- Preserve working content and behavior unless the brief calls for a change.
- Inspect your changes in the browser at representative viewport sizes, fix visible problems, and check for console errors.

Take ownership of the design details. Finish the implementation rather than stopping at recommendations or a mockup.
